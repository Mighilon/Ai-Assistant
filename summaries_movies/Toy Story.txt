Toy Story is set in a world where toys come to life whenever humans are not watching. Woody is a pull-string cowboy doll and the beloved favorite toy of a boy named Andy. Woody is confident, responsible, and the respected leader of all the toys in Andy's room. Everything changes when Andy receives a Buzz Lightyear action figure for his birthday, a sleek, impressive space ranger toy loaded with gadgets and lights. Andy quickly becomes obsessed with Buzz, and Woody feels his status as Andy's favorite slipping away. Woody's resentment of Buzz grows into open hostility, though Buzz himself is cheerfully oblivious because he genuinely believes he is a real space ranger on a mission, not a toy. During a series of mishaps fueled by Woody's jealousy, both Woody and Buzz end up stranded outside of Andy's house and must navigate the neighborhood to get back. Their greatest danger comes from Sid, a destructive boy next door who takes pleasure in torturing and dismantling toys. Captured by Sid, Woody and Buzz must work together to survive, and in doing so, they develop genuine mutual respect. Buzz finally accepts the painful truth that he is indeed a toy after seeing himself advertised on television. Rather than despair, he finds new purpose in being Andy's toy. In a dramatic chase sequence, they escape Sid and race to catch the moving truck as Andy's family relocates. The adventure ends with Woody and Buzz firmly established as best friends and equal favorites in Andy's heart.
