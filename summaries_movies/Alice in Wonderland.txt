Alice is a young, imaginative girl who grows bored while her older sister reads to her in a meadow. When a peculiar White Rabbit rushes past, muttering about being late and consulting a pocket watch, Alice's curiosity gets the better of her and she follows him. She tumbles down a deep rabbit hole and finds herself in a strange, dreamlike land called Wonderland. Trying to follow the Rabbit, Alice encounters a series of bizarre and whimsical characters and situations. She drinks a potion that shrinks her, eats a cake that makes her enormous, and struggles repeatedly to get through a tiny door that leads to a beautiful garden. Along the way she meets the excitable Dodo, the rude Tweedledee and Tweedledum, a talking Doorknob, and the mysterious Cheshire Cat, who gives her cryptic but helpful advice with a wide, floating grin. She attends a nonsensical tea party hosted by the Mad Hatter and the March Hare, where time has been permanently frozen at six o'clock. Eventually Alice reaches the garden and discovers it belongs to the tyrannical Queen of Hearts, a volatile ruler who settles every dispute by ordering beheadings. Alice is put on trial for various offenses and, when the chaos becomes overwhelming, she finally snaps and boldly stands up to the Queen. The entire court turns against her and gives chase, but Alice escapes back through the rabbit hole and wakes up in the meadow, wondering if it was all a dream.
