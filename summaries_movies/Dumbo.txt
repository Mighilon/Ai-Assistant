Dumbo is set in a traveling circus and follows a small elephant named Jumbo Jr., cruelly nicknamed Dumbo by the other elephants because of his extraordinarily large ears. His mother, Mrs. Jumbo, is fiercely protective of her unusual son, and when she lashes out at some boys who mock him, she is locked away and branded as a mad elephant. Dumbo is left alone, ridiculed by the other elephants and treated as an outcast. His only friend is Timothy Q. Mouse, a small but streetwise and confident mouse who takes pity on Dumbo and becomes his devoted champion. Timothy convinces the ringmaster to use Dumbo in a clown act in which he falls from a tall platform into a tiny tub of water. The crowd laughs but Dumbo is humiliated. One night, Dumbo and Timothy accidentally drink water spiked with champagne and wake up in a tree, high above the ground. Trying to figure out how they got there, Timothy realizes that Dumbo must have flown up using his enormous ears as wings. Timothy devises a plan and convinces Dumbo that a magic feather gives him the power to fly. During the next circus performance, Dumbo leaps from a high platform, drops the feather, panics, but then spreads his ears and soars over the astonished crowd. He becomes a sensation and a star. His mother is freed and reunited with him, and Dumbo is given a contract and a life of fame, finally celebrated for the very ears that once made him a target of ridicule.
