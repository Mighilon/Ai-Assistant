Bambi is the story of a young white-tailed deer growing up in a lush, peaceful forest. The film opens with the birth of Bambi, the son of the Great Prince of the Forest, a majestic and respected stag. The news of Bambi's arrival spreads quickly among the woodland animals, who gather to celebrate. As a fawn, Bambi is wide-eyed and unsteady on his legs, and he quickly befriends Thumper, an energetic young rabbit, and Flower, a shy little skunk. Together they explore the meadow and the forest, learning about the natural world through play and curiosity. Bambi's mother tenderly guides him through the seasons and teaches him to be cautious, especially on the open meadow where danger lurks. That danger comes in the form of Man, the unseen but ever-present threat to the forest. One winter day, while grazing in the snow, Bambi's mother is shot by a hunter and killed. Bambi is left alone and devastated, and his stoic father quietly takes him under his wing. Time passes and Bambi grows into a strong young buck. He falls in love with Faline, a doe he knew as a fawn. He must fight off a rival deer to win her affection. The greatest danger comes when hunters return, bringing dogs and starting a massive forest fire. Bambi narrowly escapes and guides Faline to safety. The film closes as seasons turn again and Faline gives birth to twin fawns, with Bambi taking his father's place as the Great Prince of the Forest.
