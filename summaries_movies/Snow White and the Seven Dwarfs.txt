Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs is Disney's first feature-length animated film and tells the classic fairy tale of a young princess threatened by a jealous queen. Snow White is a gentle and beautiful princess who lives in a castle with her vain and cruel stepmother, the Evil Queen. The Queen possesses a magic mirror that she consults daily, asking who is the fairest in the land. When the mirror declares that Snow White has surpassed the Queen in beauty, the Queen becomes consumed with envy and orders a huntsman to take Snow White into the forest and kill her, bringing back her heart as proof. The huntsman cannot bring himself to do it and urges Snow White to flee. She runs deep into the forest and finds a small, cluttered cottage belonging to seven dwarfs, Grumpy, Happy, Sleepy, Bashful, Doc, Sneezy, and Dopey, who are out mining. She tidies the cottage and falls asleep. When the dwarfs return, they are charmed by Snow White and agree to let her stay. The Queen discovers from the mirror that Snow White still lives and, disguising herself as an old peddler woman, visits the cottage with a poisoned apple. Snow White takes a bite and falls into a death-like sleep. The dwarfs, too late to save her, place her in a glass coffin in the forest. A Prince who had met Snow White before rides through, is moved by her beauty, and kisses her. The spell breaks, Snow White awakens, and she leaves with the Prince to his castle.
