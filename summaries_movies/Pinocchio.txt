Pinocchio tells the story of a wooden puppet who longs to become a real boy. An elderly wood-carver named Geppetto crafts a puppet he names Pinocchio and wishes upon a star that the puppet could come to life. That night, the Blue Fairy visits the workshop and grants Pinocchio life, telling him that if he is brave, truthful, and unselfish, he may one day become a real boy. She assigns Jiminy Cricket to serve as his conscience. The next morning, instead of going to school, Pinocchio is lured away by the sly fox Honest John and his companion Gideon. He ends up in the puppet theater of the villainous Stromboli, who plans to keep him performing forever, locked in a cage. The Blue Fairy rescues him after he lies about where he has been, causing his nose to grow longer with each fib. Despite the lesson, Pinocchio is tricked again and taken to Pleasure Island, a place where boys are encouraged to misbehave. He discovers too late that the boys who come there are slowly transformed into donkeys and sold into labor. Pinocchio narrowly escapes and returns home to find that Geppetto has gone to sea searching for him and has been swallowed by a massive whale named Monstro. Pinocchio bravely dives into the ocean, finds Monstro, and devises a plan to make the whale sneeze so they can escape. Geppetto is saved but Pinocchio appears to drown in the effort. The Blue Fairy rewards his selfless bravery by transforming him into a real boy.
