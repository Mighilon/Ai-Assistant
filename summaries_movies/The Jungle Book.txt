The Jungle Book is set in the lush jungles of India and follows Mowgli, a young boy who has been raised by wolves since he was a baby. When the fearsome man-eating tiger Shere Khan returns to their part of the jungle, the wolf pack reluctantly decides that Mowgli must be taken to a human village for his own safety. Bagheera, a wise and dignified black panther who found Mowgli as an infant, volunteers to escort him. Mowgli refuses to go, insisting the jungle is his home, and runs away from Bagheera. His journey back through the jungle brings him into contact with a colorful parade of characters. He encounters Kaa, a hypnotic python who twice tries to devour him, and a squadron of militaristic elephants led by the pompous Colonel Hathi. He also befriends the loveable, easygoing bear Baloo, who teaches Mowgli his carefree philosophy of finding the bare necessities of life and having fun. Baloo becomes so fond of Mowgli that he initially refuses to believe he must go to the village. Then Mowgli is captured by a swinging band of monkeys who bring him to their leader, King Louie, an orangutan who wants Mowgli to teach him the secret of fire. Bagheera and Baloo rescue him. When Shere Khan finally confronts Mowgli, the boy bravely stands his ground, ties a burning branch to the tiger's tail, and drives him off. A glimpse of a young girl from the village captivates Mowgli and he follows her, choosing human life at last, while Baloo and Bagheera watch him go with bittersweet pride.
