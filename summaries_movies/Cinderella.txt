Cinderella is a kind and gentle young woman who lives in misery in her own home. After her loving father remarried and then died, she was left in the care of her cold and selfish stepmother and two spoiled, cruel stepsisters, Anastasia and Drizella. Cinderella is forced to work as a servant in the household, cooking, cleaning, and catering to every whim of her stepfamily while they mock and belittle her. Despite her circumstances, Cinderella remains warm-hearted and hopeful, finding companionship in the mice and birds who live around the estate, whom she clothes and feeds and considers her true friends. When a royal invitation arrives announcing a ball where the Prince will choose a bride, Cinderella longs to attend. Her stepmother agrees to let her go if she finishes all her chores, but then deliberately gives her an impossible workload. Cinderella's animal friends help her refashion a beautiful gown from old clothes, but her stepsisters spitefully tear it to pieces. In despair, Cinderella runs to the garden and weeps, where her Fairy Godmother appears and transforms a pumpkin into a carriage, mice into horses, and Cinderella's rags into a dazzling ball gown, complete with glass slippers. She arrives at the palace and dances with the enchanted Prince, but the spell breaks at midnight and she flees, losing one glass slipper on the steps. The Prince searches the kingdom for the girl whose foot fits the slipper, eventually finding Cinderella, and the two are reunited to live happily ever after.
