The Lion King is set in the African savanna and follows Simba, a young lion cub who is heir to the Pride Lands ruled by his beloved father, King Mufasa. Mufasa's jealous and treacherous younger brother Scar desperately wants the throne for himself. Scar engineers a wildebeest stampede and lures Mufasa into a gorge to rescue Simba. Mufasa saves Simba but is left clinging to a cliff edge, where Scar throws him to his death below. Scar then tells Simba the death was his fault and urges him to run away and never return. The traumatized young cub flees into exile and Scar takes over the Pride Lands, which quickly become barren and desolate under his reckless rule. Simba grows up in a distant jungle alongside Timon, a wisecracking meerkat, and Pumbaa, a good-natured warthog, who teach him their motto of Hakuna Matata, meaning no worries. Simba tries to leave his painful past behind, but everything changes when he encounters Nala, a childhood friend who has escaped the ruined Pride Lands. She urges him to return and take his rightful place as king. Simba is still haunted by guilt until the spirit of Mufasa appears in the night sky and tells him to remember who he is. Inspired, Simba returns to confront Scar. During the confrontation, Scar admits to the pride that he killed Mufasa. Simba defeats Scar and takes the throne, the Pride Lands are restored, and Simba eventually raises his own cub, continuing the circle of life.
